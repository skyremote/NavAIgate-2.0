03 — Understanding Your Challenge

03 Understanding Your Challenge

Based on our review of the RFP and our understanding of the commercial general construction management landscape, we see the following dynamics shaping Kajima U.S.A.'s AI opportunity:

The Construction AI Landscape

Construction is one of the last major industries to undergo digital transformation at scale. According to McKinsey, it remains among the least digitised sectors globally, with productivity growth averaging just 1% annually over the past two decades. This creates a significant competitive advantage for early movers.

Fragmented Data

Data across estimating, scheduling, procurement, project controls, field operations, safety, quality, and finance exists in multiple systems and formats — a challenge, but also where AI creates the most value through unification and insight.

Multi-Entity Complexity

Operating across Kajima U.S.A., Batson-Cook Construction, and Rodgers Builders requires an AI strategy that accounts for shared capabilities alongside entity-specific needs and operational cultures.

Labour & Knowledge Challenges

An ageing workforce and skilled labour shortages make knowledge capture and transfer critical. AI can preserve institutional knowledge and accelerate onboarding of less experienced staff.

Margin Pressure

Construction margins are typically thin (2–5%). AI-driven improvements in estimating accuracy, schedule optimisation, and risk identification directly protect and improve profitability.

04 — Approach & Methodology

04 Our Approach & Methodology

Our methodology is designed around one principle: every recommendation must be actionable, measurable, and tied to business value. We align closely with the four-phase structure outlined in the RFP, with enhancements drawn from our experience delivering AI strategies for enterprise organisations.

The NavAIgate AI Strategy Framework

1. Listen & Learn

We start by understanding your business, not your technology. Executive interviews, process mapping, and data landscape assessment ensure our recommendations are grounded in your reality.

2. Discover & Evaluate

Structured workshops surface AI opportunities from every function. Each is evaluated against a consistent framework: business impact, feasibility, data readiness, and change effort.

3. Prioritise & Plan

Quantitative scoring produces a ranked opportunity matrix. We identify quick wins, foundational enablers, and strategic bets — then sequence them into a phased roadmap across 0–12 month and 12–36 month horizons.

4. Blueprint & Present

The AI Strategy Blueprint synthesises everything into a clear, executive-ready document with governance principles, success metrics, and a concrete implementation plan.

Evaluation Framework

Every identified AI opportunity is scored across five dimensions using a weighted matrix:

Dimension Weight What We Assess
Business Impact 30% Revenue uplift, cost reduction, risk mitigation, competitive advantage
Feasibility 25% Technical complexity, integration requirements, vendor maturity
Data Readiness 20% Data availability, quality, accessibility, governance requirements
Time-to-Value 15% Implementation timeline, dependencies, speed to measurable outcomes
Change Effort 10% Workflow disruption, training needs, cultural readiness, stakeholder buy-in

Workshop Approach: We facilitate structured, cross-functional workshops using a combination of process mapping, pain-point identification, and opportunity brainstorming. Participants typically include department heads, project managers, field supervisors, and technology leads. Each workshop is designed to surface both obvious and non-obvious AI opportunities.

05 — Scope of Work

05 Scope of Work & Deliverables

We propose a thorough 16-week engagement structured across four phases, with built-in checkpoints and buffer time to accommodate stakeholder availability across three business entities. This timeline reflects the reality of working with senior leaders across multiple offices and ensures the depth of analysis required to produce genuinely actionable recommendations.

Phase 1: Discovery & Current-State Assessment
Weeks 1–5

Deeply understand the strategic landscape, operational realities, data environment, and organisational readiness across all three entities. This phase is deliberately paced to allow proper access to senior stakeholders without creating scheduling pressure.

  • Executive & leadership interviews (12–15 sessions) across Kajima U.S.A., Batson-Cook, and Rodgers Builders — scheduled across 4 weeks to accommodate executive availability and allow time for reflection between conversations
  • Operational deep-dives (4–6 sessions) with mid-level managers and project teams to understand day-to-day workflows, pain points, and workarounds that leadership may not see
  • Process mapping across the full construction lifecycle: estimating, scheduling, procurement, project controls, field operations, safety, quality, finance, and talent development
  • Data landscape assessment: inventory of all systems, data flows, quality metrics, accessibility, and governance posture across entities
  • Technology platform review: existing tools, analytics capabilities, integration points, and technical debt
  • AI readiness assessment: people (skills, culture, appetite for change), process (maturity, standardisation), technology (infrastructure, data pipelines)

Deliverable: Current-State Assessment Summary including process maps, data landscape diagram, technology inventory, AI readiness scorecard, and stakeholder alignment report

Phase 2: AI Opportunity Identification & Evaluation
Weeks 5–9

Surface, catalogue, and rigorously evaluate AI opportunities across all functions and business units. Workshops are spread across 4 weeks to allow for preparation, follow-up, and iterative refinement.

  • Cross-functional workshops (6–8 sessions) covering preconstruction, operations, project controls, safety, procurement, finance, HR/talent, and corporate functions — conducted entity-by-entity, then jointly
  • Standardised evaluation of each opportunity against the five-dimension framework (business impact, feasibility, data readiness, time-to-value, change effort)
  • AI opportunity inventory with detailed descriptions, assumptions, prerequisites, estimated value ranges, and implementation considerations
  • External benchmarking: how leading construction firms (Skanska, Kiewit, Turner, etc.) and adjacent industries are deploying AI in comparable functions
  • Quick wins identification: initiatives that can deliver measurable value within 0–6 months to build momentum and organisational confidence

Deliverable: AI Opportunity Inventory & Evaluation Matrix (scored, categorised, with full supporting detail and benchmarking data)

Phase 3: Prioritisation & Roadmap Development
Weeks 9–13

Transform the opportunity inventory into a clear, ranked, and sequenced implementation roadmap that spans two planning horizons, distinguishing between quick wins, foundational enablers, and strategic transformation programmes.

  • Quantitative and qualitative ranking of all identified AI opportunities using weighted scoring, with sensitivity analysis on key assumptions
  • Three-tier categorisation:
    • Quick Wins (0–6 months): Low complexity, high visibility initiatives to build momentum and demonstrate AI value
    • Foundation & Scale (6–12 months): Initiatives requiring data preparation, platform investment, or organisational change
    • Strategic Transformation (12–36 months): Enterprise-grade AI capabilities that drive sustained competitive advantage
  • Dependency mapping: identify shared prerequisites, data foundations, capability building blocks, and sequencing constraints
  • Resource and capability analysis: what can be executed internally, what requires external support, and where to invest in building internal AI capability
  • Investment modelling: estimated costs, expected returns, and business case summaries for top-priority initiatives
  • Phased AI roadmap with clear 0–12 month and 12–36 month horizons, milestones, decision gates, and success criteria

Deliverable: Ranked & Prioritised AI Roadmap (0–12 months + 12–36 months) with dependency map, investment model, and business case summaries

Phase 4: AI Strategy Blueprint
Weeks 13–16

Synthesise all findings into a comprehensive, executive-ready strategy document and present to leadership. This phase includes review cycles with the Committee to ensure the final Blueprint fully reflects organisational priorities.

  • AI Strategy Blueprint document including: enterprise AI vision, strategic principles and objectives, prioritised roadmap (0–12 and 12–36 months), high-level implementation approach, governance framework, risk considerations, and success metrics/KPIs
  • Governance recommendations: AI ethics principles, data governance requirements, model oversight, responsible AI usage policy, and risk management framework
  • Success metrics and KPIs for each prioritised initiative, tied to business outcomes with clear measurement methodology
  • Executive presentation of findings, recommendations, and next steps to the AI Acceleration Committee and senior leadership
  • Implementation playbook for the top 3–5 recommended quick-win initiatives, including detailed specifications, resource requirements, and 90-day launch plans

Deliverable: AI Strategy Blueprint Document + Executive Presentation + Quick-Win Implementation Playbook
