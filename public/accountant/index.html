German Tax & Business Glossary

Essential terms you'll encounter. Bookmark this section for future reference.

GmbH (Gesellschaft mit beschränkter Haftung)
"Company with limited liability"
The German equivalent of an LLC. Your personal assets are protected from business debts. Requires minimum €25,000 capital (or €50,000 for certain activities). The company is a separate legal entity from you.
Stammkapital
"Share capital" / "Registered capital"
The initial capital you put into the GmbH. Legal minimum: €25,000 (only €12,500 must be paid immediately to start). Your accountant discussed €50,000-100,000 for operational flexibility. This money can be used for business operations - it's not "locked away."
Holding / Holdinggesellschaft
"Holding company"
A parent company that owns shares in other companies. Used when you have 3-5+ businesses. The Holding receives dividends from subsidiaries with favorable tax treatment (~1.5% effective tax on dividends between companies vs ~25% for personal dividends).
Steuerberater
"Tax advisor" / "Accountant"
Licensed tax professional in Germany. They handle Finanzamt filings, tax optimization, and strategic advice. Required for GmbH annual accounts.
Finanzamt
"Tax office"
The German tax authority. Your Steuerberater communicates with them on your behalf. They will assign your GmbH a tax number (Steuernummer).
Dividende
"Dividend"
Profit distribution from the GmbH to you as owner. Taxed at ~25% (Kapitalertragsteuer) when paid to individuals. This is separate from your salary.
Geschäftsführer
"Managing Director" / "CEO"
The person who runs the GmbH. This will be you. You can pay yourself a salary as Geschäftsführer, which is a business expense.
Körperschaftsteuer
"Corporate tax"
Tax on GmbH profits: ~15% federal + ~0.8% solidarity surcharge. Plus Gewerbesteuer (trade tax) ~14-17% depending on location. Total corporate tax burden: ~30%.
Gewerbesteuer
"Trade tax"
Local business tax paid to your municipality. Rate varies by city (Hebesatz). Typically 14-17% of profits. GmbHs always pay this.
Einkommensteuer
"Income tax"
Personal income tax on your salary. Progressive rates from 14% to 45%. This is why you want to minimize personal salary and keep money in the GmbH.
Betriebsausgaben
"Business expenses"
Costs that reduce taxable profit: computers, phones, internet, travel, office supplies, software subscriptions. Must be business-related ("nur related" as accountant said).
Notar
"Notary"
Required for GmbH formation. They authenticate the founding documents (Gesellschaftsvertrag) and register with Handelsregister. Cost: ~€500-1,500.
Handelsregister
"Commercial register"
Public registry where all GmbHs are listed. Your company becomes official once registered here. Registration takes 2-4 weeks.

GmbH Structure Explained

What is a GmbH?
The German "LLC" equivalent
Core Concept

A GmbH is a separate legal entity. Unlike being self-employed where YOU are the business, the GmbH is its own "person" legally. This means:

Benefits
  • Limited liability - your house/car protected
  • Lower corporate tax rate (~30% vs up to 45%)
  • Professional appearance to clients
  • Easier to bring in investors/partners
  • Can pay yourself optimal salary
  • Business expenses reduce tax
Costs/Complexity
  • €25,000 minimum capital (€12,500 upfront)
  • Annual accounting costs (~€2,000-5,000)
  • Notary fees for setup (~€500-1,500)
  • Must file annual accounts publicly
  • More administrative work
  • Double taxation on dividends
Stammkapital (Share Capital)
Your initial investment in the company
Key Number
"Genau, ich würde eine GmbH gründen. So, die GmbH gründen Sie, weil ich Stammkapital ausgeben. Also mindestens fünfzigtausend Euro."
— Accountant (37:38)
Important: Capital is NOT Locked Away

The Stammkapital can be used for business operations. You can pay salaries, buy equipment, pay rent. It's working capital, not a frozen deposit. "Das ist das Stammkapital, mit dem können Sie in der GmbH arbeiten."

GmbH Tax Structure
How corporate vs personal tax works
Tax Type Rate What It Applies To
Körperschaftsteuer (Corp tax) ~15% GmbH profits
Solidaritätszuschlag ~0.8% On corp tax
Gewerbesteuer (Trade tax) ~14-17% GmbH profits (varies by city)
Total Corporate ~30% On GmbH profits
Kapitalertragsteuer (Dividend tax) ~25% When you take dividends personally
Your salary (Einkommensteuer) 14-45% Progressive based on income
The Strategy

Keep money in GmbH (30% tax) rather than taking large salary (up to 45% tax). Only take minimum salary for living expenses. Let profits accumulate and invest through the company.

└───────┘   └───────┘   └───────┘

Holding Benefits

  • • Tax-efficient dividend flow: Dividends from subsidiary to Holding taxed at only ~1.5% (vs 25% to you personally)
  • • Asset protection: If one business fails, others are protected
  • • Centralized cash management: Holding manages money across all entities
  • • Exit planning: Easier to sell individual businesses
"Und man muss einfach dieses Geld verwalten. Die Holding kriegt aber auch dann die Dividende."

Recommendation: Start with Single GmbH

Don't set up a Holding yet. The accountant's advice: start with one GmbH for NavAIgate. Add Holding structure later if/when you have 3-5 businesses. Setting up too early = unnecessary costs and complexity.
