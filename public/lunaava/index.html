Bavarian Education System Overview

The key things you need to know

üîë Critical Point

In Bavaria, you make a major decision at the end of Grade 4 about which secondary school path. However, paths can be changed later - it's not a dead end, just requires more effort.

The Bavarian school system is recognized as one of the most rigorous in Germany. Key differences:

  • More challenging curriculum than other German states
  • Changed from G8 (12 years) to G9 (13 years) because students couldn't cover all material
  • If you move TO Bavaria from another state, might need entrance tests
  • If you move FROM Bavaria to another state, generally no problem
  • Each German state has different education rules - what applies in Berlin doesn't apply here

Secondary School Types Explained

What happens after Grundschule (Grade 4)

The academic high school track. Leads to Abitur (university entrance qualification).

  • Highest academic track - "all doors open"
  • 9 years after Grundschule (Grade 5-13)
  • Required GPA: 2.33 average in German, Maths, HSU (or 3.33 for international families)
  • Can go to: University, Fachhochschule, Apprenticeship - any path
  • SIS offers Science Gymnasium (physics, chemistry, biology focus)
  • Also offers IB (International Baccalaureate) as optional add-on
"If a child wants to be an astronaut or engineer, they must go to Gymnasium."
‚Äî Principal Asan, SIS Ingolstadt

Secondary school track. Leads to Mittlerer Schulabschluss (MSA).

  • 6 years after Grundschule (Grade 5-10)
  • Required GPA: 2.66 average
  • Can transition to FOS after, then to Fachhochschule
  • "Some doors closed" - harder path to university but not impossible
  • More practical orientation than Gymnasium

Basic secondary school. Leads to Hauptschulabschluss or Quali.

  • No specific GPA requirement to enter
  • Grade 5-9, can extend to Grade 10 (M-Zweig)
  • Prepares for apprenticeships (bakery, store, trade work)
  • "More doors closed" - path to university is very long and difficult
  • Covers fewer topics (e.g., 40 topics vs Gymnasium's 100)

Specialized secondary school. Path to Fachhochschule or even Abitur.

  • FOS = Fachoberschule (after Realschule)
  • BOS = Berufsoberschule (after apprenticeship)
  • Can lead to Fachhochschulreife (Grade 12) or Abitur (Grade 13)
  • Allows university study in certain subject areas

SIS Primary School (Grundschule) For Luna

Grades 1-4 details

üéØ Key Features
  • Bilingual teaching: Subjects alternate between German and English each year
  • Example: Grade 5 = 4 hours Maths in English + 1 hour German. Grade 6 = opposite
  • Reason: Students learn subject vocabulary in both languages (Kreis = Circle)
  • Full-day school: Students finish homework at school, go home free
  • No grades in Grade 1, half of Grade 2 (same as Montessori)
  • Grades start: Second half of Grade 2

Every week, you receive a summary of what was taught. Principal Asan's philosophy:

"If your daughter did addition, you'll read the summary and ask 'What is 3+7?' If she answers, you're satisfied. If she can't answer multiple times, the bell should ring and you talk to the teacher. This closes the communication gap."

This is different from state schools where you get yearly plans (long documents) but no weekly visibility.

For students who want to go to STATE Gymnasium/Realschule after Grade 4:

  • Fixed hour in timetable from Grade 3 specifically for exam practice
  • Another fixed hour in Grade 4
  • 79% pass rate last year (very high for any school)
  • SIS handles all logistics - transport, preparation, everything
  • Exam is 3 days of standardized testing

Note: If staying at SIS Gymnasium, no Probeunterricht needed - they know the student already.

üîÑ Language Alternation Explained

Maths is maths in any language - the concepts are the same. But vocabulary differs. A "Kreis" is a "Circle". By alternating the teaching language each year, students become fluent in subject terminology in BOTH languages. This is critical if you ever move countries.
